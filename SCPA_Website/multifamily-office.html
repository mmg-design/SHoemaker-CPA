Multifamily Office

Your CPA and financial advisor should be in the same room.

Most people have a CPA who files their return and an advisor who manages their portfolio — and neither talks to the other. At Shoemaker CPA, Luke and CFP Jesse Stacy operate as one coordinated team, so every decision accounts for the full picture.

See how we work
The problem

Fragmented advice is expensive.

01

Tax decisions made without investment context

Your CPA sees your income and structures your return. But if your advisor is moving money without that context — selling positions, rebalancing, taking gains — the tax consequences arrive as a surprise.

02

Advisor recommendations that don't account for your tax situation

A sound investment move can be the wrong move when your effective rate, deduction strategy, and income timing aren't part of the conversation. Most advisors never see the full return.

03

Year-end surprises that better planning would have prevented

Roth conversions, business distributions, estate events — each carries tax implications that compound when your advisors aren't aligned. By December, your options are limited.

How it works

One team. Every decision, coordinated.

Luke Shoemaker and Jesse Stacy don't hand off your file — they work it together. Every recommendation considers your tax position, wealth plan, business interests, and family goals before anything moves.

Is this right for you

We work best with people who are tired of managing two advisors.

This is probably a fit if…

You've felt the disconnect between what your CPA knows and what your advisor is doing — and something got missed.

Your financial life has complexity — business income, investments, family estate considerations — that doesn't fit neatly into one box.

You want your advisors to do the coordinating — not you, not an intermediary, not an annual "check-in."

You're approaching or navigating a significant financial event — a sale, inheritance, transition — and want a team in place before it happens.

This probably isn’t for you if…

Your financial picture is relatively straightforward — a W-2, one retirement account, no business interests — and a single-point advisor serves you well.

You prefer transactional relationships — one call at filing time, one portfolio review per year, no ongoing engagement.

You're looking for the lowest-cost option — our model is an investment in depth and access, not a commodity service.

You already have a coordinated advisory team you trust — we're not here to displace something that's working.
